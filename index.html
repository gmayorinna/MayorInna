Social computing · AI governance · Digital strategy

Hi, I’m Mayor Inna Gurung.

I study how narratives form, mutate, and compete in online information environments, and what that means for society. My work combines computational methods with social theory to understand how harmful content spreads, how bias can be encoded into AI systems, and how digital platforms reshape public reasoning and communication.

A core commitment of my research is translating rigorous evidence into actionable insights for policymakers, technology teams, and public-interest organizations.

I am currently an intern with the World Bank’s Technology Strategy & Governance team, supporting initiatives related to enterprise architecture, digital transformation, and technology investment strategy. My background spans social computing research, full-stack software development, and applied technology strategy.

Research

My research sits at the intersection of social computing, misinformation studies, AI governance, and computational social science. I am especially interested in how narratives, visual symbols, and platform affordances shape engagement, trust, emotion, and information diffusion.

Current areas of interest include stance-based diffusion modeling, social media-driven information campaigns, visual semiotics, misinformation detection, and responsible AI systems.
